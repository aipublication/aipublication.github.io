We write set $A$ as proper subset of set $B$ (notation $A \subset B$). If $A$ is a subset of $B$ and there is an element of $B$ not present in $A$.

























































































































































































































































































































































































































$$\forall x(x \in A \to x \in B) \wedge \exists x(x \in B \wedge x \notin A)$$
